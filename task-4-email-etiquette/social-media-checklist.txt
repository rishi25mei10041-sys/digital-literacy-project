Social Media Do’s and Don’ts for College Students

DO’s:
•	Keep Privacy Settings Updated: Regularly check who can see your posts and personal information to maintain digital safety.
•	Think Before You Post: Consider if your content reflects a professional image that an employer or professor would respect.
•	Verify Information: Fact-check news or links before sharing them to prevent the spread of misinformation.
•	Network Professionally: Use platforms like LinkedIn to follow industry leaders and join academic groups related to AI/ML.
•	Use Strong Passwords: Protect your social accounts with Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) to prevent identity theft.
DON’Ts:
•	Don't Share Sensitive Info: Never post your registration number, home address, or OTPs on public platforms.
•	Don't Engage in Cyberbullying: Avoid inflammatory comments, harassment, or sharing unverified rumours about peers.	
•	Don't Plagiarize Content: Avoid posting others' creative work or code without giving proper credit or citations.
•	Don't Over-Post During Class: Avoid distracted social media use that interferes with your academic responsibilities.
•	Don't Use Offensive Language: Avoid using profanity or hate speech that could violate college conduct codes.
